Homework 8



As climate change accelerates the melting of the Polar Ice Caps, the Arctic Ocean will increasingly be open for resource extraction by countries and corperations. The exploitation and extraction of all resources are governed by the United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea (UNCLS). For this map I went over two of the main categories under (UNCLS), the first being territorial waters and the second being exlusive economic zones (EEZs). Territorial water extend for 12 nautical miles from a country's coastline and represent the areas in which stateshave full jursidiction. EEZ's extend 200 miles from a countries coast and represent the area in which countries have exlusive rights to natural resources in and below the surface of the water, but where all vessels are granted free passage in peace time.

For this project I used a combination of buffers and subtractions. Originally I added a shapefile containing all of the countries of the world. Next, using the selection tool I selected the countries that are in the Northern Hemisphere and that I intedned to have in my map. Usingthe selected countries only I created a buffer of 13.8 miles, which is roughly equivlent to 12 nautical miles. This buffer I created a buffer around the entire border of a county, including areas on land. This buffer layer additonally, covered all of the land of the country. In order to only see the territorial waters I subtracted the layer containing all of countries from the newly created buffer layer, leaving me with a layer which only showed a strip of water 12 nautical miles off of land. I repreated this same process to create an EEZ layer. Finally, I used a dissolve method to combine all of the polygons into open to show the total extent of water occupied by territorial waters and EEZs.
